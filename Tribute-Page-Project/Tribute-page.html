Linus Torvalds

Creator of Linux, Git, and Subsurface.

Here's is a timeline of notible events in Linus Torvalds' life so far.

  • December 28, 1969 - Linus Torvalds was born in Helsinki, Finland.
  • 1981 - Linus began getting interested in computers at age 11 with a Commodore VIC-20. Initially, he programmed for it in BASIC.
  • 1988 - Linus Torvalds started attending the University of Helsinki.
  • Summer 1989 - Linus' college career was interrupted when he joined the Finnish Navy Nyland Brigade after his first year of study. This was mandatory military service of Finland.
  • 1989 - During his service, Linus bought the book "Operating Systems: Design and Implementation" written by Andrew Tanenbaum.
  • 1990 - Linus resumes his studies at UH, where he was introduced to UNIX for the first time with a DEC MicroVAX running ULTRIX. His MSc thesis title was "Linux: A Portable Operating System"
  • January 5, 1991 - Linus purchased an Intel 80386-based clone of an IBM PC. This allowed him to begin his work on Linux.
  • 1991 - Linux prototypes were released publicly.
  • March 14, 1994 - Linux version 1.0 was released.
  • 1997-1999 - Linus helped select the standard binary format for Unix and Linux. In 1999, he was named by the MIT Technology Review TR100 as one of the top 100 innovators in the world under 35.
  • April 3, 2005 - Linus began developing Git, a version control software that is widely used today.
  • July 26. 2005Linus turned over Git's maintenance to Junio Hamano.
  • 2011 - Linus began working on Subsurface, a software to log and plan scuba dives.
  • October 22, 2018 - Linus returned to maintaining the Linux kernel. He is sponsored by the Linux Foundation, enabling him to work on Linux full-time.

If you would like to read more about Linus Torvalds, check out his Wikipedia entry.
